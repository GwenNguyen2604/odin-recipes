Scotch Egg

Ingredients

  • oil for frying
  • 6 cold large eggs
  • 21 ounces Italian sausage (3.5 oz per egg)
  • ¼ teaspoon dry mustard
  • 1 pinch freshly grated nutmeg
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 1 stalk green onion, chopped
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs, or as needed
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour, or as needed
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • For the Aioli:
  • 4 tbs. mayo
  • 2 tbs. Dijon mustard
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper

Directions

  1. Bring 1/2-inch water in a saucepan to a boil. Carefully place cold eggs into saucepan, cover with a lid, reduce heat to medium-high, and cook for about 6 minutes. Remove from heat and pour cold water over eggs to halt the cooking process. Cool eggs to room temperature in cold water. Peel and dry eggs on paper towels.
  2. Mix sausage, mustard, nutmeg, cayenne pepper, and green onion together in a bowl. Shape into 6 equal-size balls.
  3. Lay a piece of plastic wrap on a flat work surface. Place 1 sausage ball onto the center of the wrap, fold the wrap over sausage, and flatten into an 1/8-inch-thick oval shape. Pull plastic wrap back and place 1 egg in the center of the sausage. Fold and press sausage around egg to cover completely using moistened fingertips.
  4. Repeat with remaining eggs and sausage.
  5. Place breadcrumbs in a shallow bowl. Pour flour into another shallow bowl.
  6. Gently press eggs into flour to coat; shake off excess flour. Dip eggs into the beaten egg, then press into breadcrumbs. Place the breaded eggs onto a plate.
  7. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Working in batches, cook eggs in the preheated oil until golden, 5 to 6 minutes. Transfer to a wire rack to cool, at least 5 minutes.
  8. In a small bowl, compare the aioli ingredients together. Enjoy the eggs with aioli.
